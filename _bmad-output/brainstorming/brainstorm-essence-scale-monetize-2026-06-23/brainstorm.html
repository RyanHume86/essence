Essence

the day the to-do list grew into something that grows back

6 techniques partner mode pleased, not hyped status: complete

The question on the table was a commercial one — how do we make Essence something many people use, and pay for? The answer that surfaced was not. It was about a feeling at the seam between two tasks, and a companion you make yourself who is quietly glad you showed up.

1

Six Thinking Hats

circling the real problem
White
To-do apps are used a couple of sessions, then overlooked.
Red
At the seam, the wanted feeling: “I’ve achieved something.”
Green
A smile, a “well done” — a warm companion, not a taskmaster.
You drift away precisely when you’re in flow — caught up, one task spilling into the next, never needing to look at the app. the founder

The disengagement moment is success. So the app can’t fight flow — it has to live at the seams between tasks.

And what you want at that seam isn’t the checkbox tick. It’s watching something you care about visibly move — the dent the day made in a bigger goal. That’s the thing that brings you back tomorrow.

2

Mind Mapping

from a feeling to a shape

Praise has to be earned and true, tied to real movement, or it curdles into a hollow Clippy. The companion remembers what you’re working toward — and when you’ve been away, there is no “where were you.” The plant napped; it didn’t die.

Monetize the plumbing, never the warmth. You pay to keep a companion who knows you — not to be allowed to feel cared for.

3

Time Horizon Ladder

1 year · 10 years · 100 years
1year
Word-of-mouth spreads like wildfire — as many people as possible using it to the fullest. And the founder “would like to say yes,” he’d still open it daily.
10years
A companion people have created and kept for a decade — an ownership too personal to walk away from.
100years
A different default for software’s relationship to us: warm, unhurried, on your side — never ashamed of you.
The hesitation is the whole ballgame. If the founder isn’t sure he’d open it daily, that’s the retention cliff. Wildfire can’t be manufactured — it is downstream of genuine daily pull. Crack “what makes me open it unhesitatingly,” and virality follows.

And the sharpened claim the product must earn: “I actually want to do my tasks.”

4

Entanglement Thinking

one idea, three jobs

The biggest idea of the session: you create and customize your own companion. First thirty seconds: make your character → instant ownership. And because you can’t ditch something you made, that same single decision does three jobs at once.

Job one
Make it in 30s — you’re hooked
Job two
You built it — you won’t leave
Job three
“I made mine” — you tell a friend

Around this one loop sit its amplifiers: a warm signature sound/voice (half the feeling, ignored by every to-do app); a hidden dependency — movement needs a Care to move, so progress means something; proportional warmth scaled to the size of the win (anti-hype); and an opt-in, shareable milestone card as the organic word-of-mouth engine.

The synthesis

Essence is a visible-progress companion you create yourself — pleased not hyped, growing the Cares you choose, that never shames you and lives for one sacred 30-second win.

Free at heart; pay to carry it everywhere. The plumbing, never the warmth.
