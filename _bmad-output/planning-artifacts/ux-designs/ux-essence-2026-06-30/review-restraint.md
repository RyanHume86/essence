# Verdict

**PASS WITH CONDITIONS.** The spines are unusually disciplined and the restraint contract is mostly enforced *by construction* rather than by policy — the strongest possible form. No hard blockers that ship shame, streaks, or the wall-of-tasks. But there are two should-fix items where a loophole is left open (the wheel's "keeps counting" framing and the carry-forward highlight's failure to specify a hard ceiling), plus several nits where microcopy or under-specification could quietly reintroduce pressure at build time. Fix the should-fixes before they calcify into code.

# Cross-spine contradictions

The wheel description is consistent across both files (same bans listed) — the weakness is shared, not contradictory.

# Loopholes that pass today but a future feature could exploit

1. **The wheel's "keeps counting" is a denominator-in-waiting.** Today there's no number; the moment anyone adds a target, goal, or "full" state to grow engagement, the ring becomes a streak/quota with zero new components — the geometry is already there (`RING_TARGET=8` exists). This is the single highest-risk loophole. Hard-spec "no closure, no full state."
2. **Accumulation reframed as a goal needs no new UI.** "Grow the ring" / "your ring today" copy or a year-in-review of rings would weaponize an element that passes the letter of FR25. Counter-metric ("anxiety-on-open") should be cited at the wheel, not just at the page level.
3. **Carry-forward stacking is unbanned.** Per-task non-escalation is locked; aggregate volume is not. A future "X tasks carried over" summary, or carried tasks bubbling to the top of Plan, would re-encode backlog dread (the exact counter-metric) without touching the per-task rule.
4. **The "nudge to decompose" is a pressure beachhead.** A future "tasks without subtasks" filter, count, or reminder grows naturally from an un-fenced nudge and slides toward prescribing structure (rule 4).
5. **Notifications opt-in toggle is the re-engagement vector.** NFR7 keeps it off-by-default, but the toggle's existence is the door through which "you've been gone" (rule 3) could later arrive as a "gentle reminder" feature. Worth a standing note that any notification content is itself bound by rules 2 and 3 — no absence/shame copy even if the user opts in.
6. **Archive lacks a visual guardrail.** Structurally banned in one file only; a visual contributor working from DESIGN.md alone has no fence against date-tiles or counts.
